The Association of Irish Celtic Supporters Clubs was formed in April 1998 as Celtic closed in on finally ending the domestic dominance of Rangers by claiming their first title in a decade.

The idea behind the A.I.C.S.C was to finally bring together the many Celtic supporters clubs based throughout the whole of Ireland. Naturally the idea proved to be a huge success with club's finally having the opportunity to pool resources and follow the Bhoys with much greater frequency then previously possible.

Delegate meetings are held on a province-by-province rota basis. the association also has its very own succesful magazine - The View from the 32 - which promotes both Celtic and the A.I.C.S.C. An Annual Charity Dinner is the highlight of the A.I.C.S.C social calendar and the event has in the past welcomed a whole host of past Celtic greats.
